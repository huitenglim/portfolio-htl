About Me

Hey, there. I'm Lim.

And I'm a front-end developer with more than 3 years of experience. I'm currently working remotely in Cork, Ireland. Before the pandemic, I used to work in the office. Though it is something that I still enjoy immensely, I do find great comfort in working from home. My field of focus leans towards front-end web development where I work with JavaScript on a day-to-day basis.

I have experience working with Backbone.js, Angular and Ionic. In terms of back-end and databases, I have basic knowledge working with MEAN (MongoDB, ExpressJS,Angular and Node.js) stack, Firebase and MySQL. Recently, I have decided to extend my knowledge in React.js.

My primary exposure to web development started in college when I was introduced to the world of OOP using PHP, which was also my first programming language. Since then, I have started self-learning JavaScript. As someone who has an extreme fondness towards design, I've always enjoyed working with projects with a mixture of artistic and technical aspects. Being a front-end developer allows me to connect them together and it is something that I really appreaciate a lot.

Melontoon

  • Framework: Laravel
  • Scope: Web Application & CMS
Melontoon is a web application built with the expressive Laravel framework. The aim of developing this website is ultimately to provide readers from all around the globe to read their favourite comic in their own language. Scanlating and translating a comic takes effort and at times the duration of the process might take longer than expected, particularly for certain titles with a smaller fanbase. Melontoon has a mini translation tool built-in the app to ease the frustration of many readers from waiting ages just to be able to read their favourite comic. Any registered users who range from somewhat familiar to competent in the original language of the particular comic are given the opportunity to contribute by translating some of the titles and share the joy with the world. The tool is simple and straightforward to use where text can be annotated directly on the panel.
